Your app works fine… until it suddenly returns the wrong user’s data.

No errors.

No crashes.

Just silent, dangerous bugs.

This is what a captive dependency looks like in ASP .NET Core.

The mistake?

You create a Singleton service. Then inject a Scoped dependency into it.

Looks harmless.

But here is what actually happens:

- The Singleton is created once
  
- The Scoped service is injected once
  
- That instance gets reused forever

And its effect would be:

- You lose the scoped lifecycle
  
- State leaks across requests
  
- Thread-safety issues appear
  
- You may hit subtle bugs under load

So your Scoped service is trapped (captive) inside the Singleton.

It can’t behave per request anymore.

Solution?

Inject IServiceScopeFactory and call CreateScope to get your required scoped dependency

IServiceScopeFactory is part of the built-in DI engine of ASP .NET Core:

- Created internally by the container
  
- Used to create scopes (CreateScope())
  
- Always available for injection
